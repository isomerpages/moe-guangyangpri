<p>Our <strong>Digital Explorers</strong> programme introduces Primary 1 to
4 students to technology through hands-on experiences with computers and
tablets. Students discover fundamental digital skills whilst exploring
how these tools can enhance creativity, communication, and learning. Through
engaging activities, they develop digital literacy that empowers them to
become self-directed learners who can independently seek information, solve
problems, and express ideas using technology. This foundational programme
builds curiosity and confidence for lifelong learning in our digital world.</p>
<h4><strong>Developing Technology Skills</strong></h4>
<p>&nbsp;<strong>Primary 1</strong>
</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 75%;" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Signature Programmes/Infocomm/ICT_2026_2.png">
</div>
<p>• Logins and passwords</p>
<p>• Student Learning Space</p>
<p>• Touch typing</p>
<p>Students learn the use of these within the context of their English Lessons,
applying these skills to complete tasks within their English Curriculum
and using these skills in conjunction with their learning of the English
Language.</p>
<p>Our Primary 1 students were introduced to essential cybersecurity foundations,
learning how to create strong, secure passwords and safely navigate the
login process for the Student Learning Space. Additionally, they developed
fundamental touch typing skills that will serve as the building blocks
for their future digital literacy. These foundational ICT skills ensure
our students are well-equipped to engage confidently and safely with technology
as they progress through their educational journey.</p>
<p></p>
<p><strong>Primary 2</strong>
</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 75%;" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Signature Programmes/Infocomm/4.jpg">
</div>
<p>• Google Classroom assignments - Retrieving and submitting assignments</p>
<p>• Google Documents</p>
<p>• Google Slides</p>
<p>Students continue their use of these tools within their English Language
learning, utilising the storage capabilities of google drive to store and
continually work on their work. At the end of Primary 2 students submit
a written work of their own to their teachers via google classroom.</p>
<p>Our Primary 2 students worked on mastering the basics of Google Classroom.
They learnt to navigate the platform and use Google Docs to create, edit,
and submit assignments on their own. The students were also introduced
to Google Slides, where they created short presentations to showcase their
English writing. These digital tools helped develop both their technical
skills and their ability to communicate and express ideas creatively.</p>
<p></p>
<p><strong>Primary 3</strong>
</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 75%;" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Signature Programmes/Infocomm/ICT_2026_3_png.jpg">
</div>
<p>• Google Documents - collaborative aspects, sharing and editing</p>
<p>• Google Slides - collaborative work on presentation slides</p>
<p>• Google classroom</p>
<p>Students at Primary 3 learning new skills and their use within the context
of Social Studies, deploying these skills to actively collaborate with
each other on presentation slides and using google docs to collaboratively
work on scripting.&nbsp; The tasks employed are to encourage students to
use technology to work collaboratively, developing their 21st Century Skills
as well as technology tools that enable collaborative work remotely.&nbsp;
These form the foundation for technology enabled learning.</p>
<p>Students at Primary 3 advanced their collaborative skills by working together
on Google Documents and Google Slides. They applied these digital tools
to complete a social studies project as a team, learning to share ideas,
edit documents collectively, and create presentations together. This collaborative
approach not only strengthened their technical proficiency but also developed
important teamwork and communication skills essential for their academic
growth.</p>
