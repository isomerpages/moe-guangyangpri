<h3>ICT Committee Goal</h3>
<p>In alignment with the school's vision - <strong>A Vibrant School Leaders of Character, Learners for Life</strong>,
the Infocomm Technology Curriculum aims to produce Digitally Empowered,
Future-ready Learners and Innovators. This takes point from the Ministry
of Education's overall<a href="https://www.moe.gov.sg/education-in-sg/educational-technology-journey/edtech-masterplan" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"> <u>EdTech Plan.</u></a>
</p>
<p>The Digitally Empowered, Future-ready Innovators Curriculum (DEFI) in
Townsville Primary School provides our students with progressive equipping
of technology skills and development of their<a href="https://www.moe.gov.sg/education-in-sg/educational-technology-journey/edtech-masterplan/digital-literacy-and-technological-skills" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"> <u>digital competencies</u></a>.
In line with the school's ethos of preparing learners for life, ICT skills
are taught to students to equip them with the necessary InfoComm skills
to engage learning. The planned exposure and skills are designed to ignite
their learning and allow them the means to maximise their potential not
just as consumers of technology but as creators with technology.</p>
<p>Our twin goals:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Digitally and technologically literate learners who are self-directed
in learning</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Technology-enabled creators, collaborators and connectors<strong>.</strong>
</p>
</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Primary 4 - To be implemented in 2026</strong>
</p>
<p>Upper Primary ICT Lessons:
<br>The upper primary series of ICT lessons will focus on equipping our students
to be producers versus consumers of content.
<br>
<br>It will focus on giving them basic skills in creation of content on platforms
such as video and adobe, providing them with opportunities in video production
and editing, use of tools such as photoshop.&nbsp; These take place within
the subject areas of Art and Music.</p>
<p><strong>For Primary 5</strong>
<br>Video editing and use of photoshop.&nbsp; Music making with apps such
as Garageband.</p>
<p><strong>For Primary 6</strong>
<br>Coding for Fun - The Code For Fun (CFF) Enrichment Programme is a national-mandated
programme that is offered to Primary 6 students in TVPS. Through this 10-hour
standardised curriculum, students are exposed to coding and computational
thinking and their applications to daily lives. Students will undergo sessions
that allow them to understand basic programming concepts and develop logical
thinking and problem-solving skills. They are given opportunities to apply
their knowledge gained through an activity where they will individually
programme a dance using their Sphero robotic kits. Students are also introduced
to basic concepts of emerging technologies such as Artificial Intelligence
and cybersecurity concepts.</p>
<p>Coding for Fun is the finale programme that exposes students to programming.</p>
